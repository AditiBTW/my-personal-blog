My Experience with Reactive Programming [Part -1]

In this blog post, I'll be sharing my learnings on building microservices using concepts of Reactive Programming.
My previous project was based on a business use case where a service was expected to act as a core capability to be consumed by other services thereby improving user experience.

Business Background

Existing business has different product services with their respective user journeys. There are some steps in those user journeys which can be developed as a core component as they are replicated across different products and partner services.

Business Use case

When working towards providing a seamless user experience to end-customers and for other partners, the business faces following hurdles:

  • Existing architecture design makes it difficult to intergrate with new partners and products while catering to exising users.
  • Repetitive steps across user journeys makes it difficult to effectively update / enhance those steps for uniform user experience across products.

Business requirements

  • Improve onboarding for new business partners by having an easily configurable setup.
  • To develop microservices which can be scaled independently.(irrespective partner and products)
  • Extract common steps in existing application
    • To create a microservice which can be developed as a core component consumed by respective products and services
    • To enhance created microservice which helps reflect enhancements across respective services.
    • To independently upgrade products and services for different business partners.
  • The application can be in future developed and enhanced to facilitate business partners in directly consuming the microservice components.
