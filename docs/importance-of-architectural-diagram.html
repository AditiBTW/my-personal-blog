Importance of making an architectural design before jumping on to coding?

In this blog post, I'll be sharing my learnings on why it is important to make architectural design before coding.

What happens when we don't?

      Introduces following difficulties:
    • Unable to track the communication of the major technical decisions to team.
    • Losing records of why particular way / method was introduced i.e. important techincal consideration made over time in response to various technical setbacks.
    • Unclear & Undefined boundaries and interactions between different system components.
    • Inability to document the architectural enhancements made on the system/product being developed.
    • Unable to record the communication with key stakeholders on the future enhancement and scalablility of system/ product.
    • Unable to demonstrate /showcase, the system overview to business owners and other stakeholders.
    • Inefficient system interactions can result in introducing bugs and unnecessary behaviours in system/product.
    • Lack of sound reasoning of system design can also result in challenges while onboarding new joinees.
Now that we've seen the dire consequences of not having done the architectural designing prior to coding, let's check what advantages

Benefits of architectural design

  • Helps think through components that are a part of the problem statement/ business use case.
    • users
      • end user: end-customers
      • business: owners, attendents
      • system: automated, async, sync, third party.. eg notification, authentication
    • services
  • Understand the boundaries of various components of the system.
  • Interaction of various components of system, in terms of different services and user journeys.
  • Define a clear picture so the relations between various classes and components structure is well understood.
  • Helps in giving a glimpse of the system architecture evolution journey over time to onboarding team members.
  • document key technical events in response to various setbacks or client demands
    • Identify technical decisions in response to:
      • Monolith vs Microservices architectures.
      • Domain boundaries of services/ components.
      • Communication style and patterns between various components of system.
      • Expected behaviour with various users of system/product.
      • Type of data generated and consumed across the components
      • Databases needed to store revelant data to ensure proper functioning
      • Identify points of system failures and find ways to overcome them
        • Address them according to degrees of failure
      • Infrastructre considerations.
      • Understand pros and cons of various approaches
        • Event-driven
        • gRPC
        • Rest
      • Pattern to follow to gain best advantage of microservice architecture.
      • Understand user flow
        • success
        • failure
        • update/modify
        • delete/remove
  • Demonstrate progress of architectural enhancements with stakeholders and business owners.
  • Helps check understanding of business use cases with key stakeholders
    • Assists in better knowledge sharing of the associated interaction of components with stake holders.
    • Helps identify which pain points needs to be addressed (immediately and can be settled later [put into backlog])
    • Verify understanding of how the system is expected to behave with stakeholders.
    • Uncover various blind spots
    • Undersand the scale and magnitude of system
      • Associated stages in which system/product can be build to incorporate features to address business pain points
      Some examples:
    • system for raising ticket (helpdesk)
    • booking system (hotel, movie, transport- road/water/air ways, ...)
